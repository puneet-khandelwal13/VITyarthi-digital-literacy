Overview of Projects

The Digital Literacy Project seeks to cultivate core digital abilities needed in academic, vocational, and online settings. Acting as a Digital Ambassador in this project, the student is in charge of helping colleagues to use digital tools responsibly, keep a professional internet presence, and guarantee safe digital behavior.

Five modules make up the project; each targets a different area of digital literacy including awareness, creation of an online portfolio, platform use, communication etiquette, and cybersecurity. Alongside a thorough project report, all tasks are gathered into a GitHub repository.

The aim is to give hands-on exposure to often used digital tools, sharpen communication skills, and raise awareness of online dangers and obligations. Module Summaries

Module 1: Knowledge of Digital Literacy

This module concentrates on grasping the meaning of digital literacy and its significance. Creating an infographic defining important issues like safe internet practices, digital tools, professional presence, and email etiquette is the task. It improves awareness of responsible digital behavior and promotes creative thinking.

----

Module 2: Student Electronic Portfolio

This module stresses creating a professional internet presence. Profiles on GitHub and LinkedIn help students to demonstrate their scholastic background and interests. It clarifies how businesses and institutions use digital portfolios.

---

Module 3: Coding and Collaboration Platforms

This module provides hands-on coding platform and cloud-based tool exposure. Students generate a Google Form for cooperation after finishing a novice programming challenge. It builds knowledge of tools employed in academic collaboration and sharpens problem-solving abilities.

---

Module 4: Email Etiquette and Communication

Professional communication skills are the emphasis of this module. Students develop a checklist for responsible social media use and formal email drafts. It emphasizes how crucial tone, structure, and clarity are in electronic correspondence.

---

Module 5: Cybercrime Consciousness

This module helps one to become aware of online dangers including identification theft, fraud, and phishing. Students develop a prevention checklist and a case study, therefore enabling them to recognize risks and follow secure digital behaviors.

-

Combining technical skills, communication, and cybersecurity awareness, these modules offer a complete basis in digital literacy.
