Case Study: UPI, online payment fraud

A frequent cybercrime called UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud involves hackers persuading users to permit illegal money transfers. Instead of technical weaknesses, it takes advantage of users' trust and ignorance.

Often impersonating a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support representative, the con artist first reaches the victim over phone, SMS, or messenger apps. Step 1: The attacker establishes urgency (e.g., “Your account will be blocked” or “You have received a refund”). Second step: The victim is invited to share a QR code or click a malicious link. Step 3: The assailant directs the victim to “approve” a request on a UPI app such Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Step 4: The victim unwittingly approves a payment instead of getting money. Step 5: Recovering is made challenging when funds are instantaneously sent to the attacker's account.

The main targets are senior citizens, students, and those not acquainted with digital payment systems. High transaction volumes also make small company owners often targets.

Immediate financial loss, emotional stress, and possible misuse of personal information are among the results. In extreme situations, frequent scams could erode faith in virtual systems. This underlines how important awareness is in never accepting random payment demands and checking sources before acting.
